Where History Meets Elegance

Vienna, the capital of Austria, stands as a testament to centuries of art, music, and culture. From the grandeur of the Habsburg Empire to the birthplace of classical music, Vienna has always been a city that moves to its own rhythm—a rhythm that finds its perfect expression in the waltz.

The Imperial City

Architectural Marvels and Timeless Beauty

Schönbrunn Palace

The former imperial summer residence, a UNESCO World Heritage site, stands as a symbol of Baroque architecture and imperial power. Its 1,441 rooms tell stories of emperors and empresses who shaped European history.

St. Stephen's Cathedral

Rising 137 meters above the city, this Gothic masterpiece has watched over Vienna for over 700 years. Its intricate stonework and colorful tiled roof create a stunning silhouette against the Austrian sky.

The Ringstrasse

This grand boulevard, built on the site of the old city walls, showcases Vienna's finest architecture. Museums, theaters, and palaces line this 5.3-kilometer circular road, creating an open-air museum of historic grandeur.

Our Waltz

The Viennese waltz, with its smooth, continuous 3/4 rhythm, is more than a historic ballroom tradition—it’s a reflection of us. Its elegance lies in the way two partners move in effortless harmony, anticipating each other’s steps, supporting each other’s turns, and gliding through space as one. In its flowing motion and perfect synchronization, I see the way we navigate life together: balanced, connected, and always in tune, no matter how fast the world spins around us.

Our Plan Unfolded

The Grand Ferdinand

Step inside and you’re greeted with a sense of refined celebration—rooftop views stretching over the city’s historic skyline, inviting salons, and a spirit of old-world hospitality reimagined for today. 02

City Exploration

Together, we’ll wander through Vienna like characters in our own timeless story—hand in hand along the grand boulevards, slipping into quiet courtyards, and lingering in cafés where the scent of coffee and warm pastries fills the air. We’ll explore museums that hold centuries of art and history, discover hidden streets that feel untouched by time, and let the city’s music guide us from one beautiful moment to the next. Whether we’re tasting traditional dishes, strolling through palace gardens, or simply watching the city move around us, Vienna will unfold for us piece by piece—an experience rich, magical, and entirely ours to enjoy.

03

Giselle

At the Vienna State Opera, Giselle unfolds like a whispered legend brought to life—haunting, romantic, and steeped in the full magic of Romantic ballet. Beneath the soft glow of the stage, the story drifts between the warmth of a sunlit village and the moonlit realm of the Wilis, where love, betrayal, and forgiveness blend into something otherworldly. The dancers move with ethereal precision, their bright costumes shimmering against muted, dreamlike sets, each motion carrying the emotional weight of a tale where passion transcends life itself. As the orchestra breathes life into Adam’s timeless score, the audience is swept into Giselle’s world—captivated by her innocence, shaken by her heartbreak, and ultimately moved by a love strong enough to defy even death. It’s an experience that lingers long after the curtain falls, a piece of Vienna’s artistic soul shared in one unforgettable night.

04

And then there's... us.

And then there’s the gentle coziness of simply being together again—unhurried, unburdened, and wrapped in the comforts Vienna seems to offer at every corner. We’ll slip into warm, wood-paneled cafés where time slows down, savoring rich pastries, velvety coffees, and long conversations that feel like home. Dinner will become its own little ritual: candlelit tables, comfortingly hearty dishes, and that quiet feeling of closeness that comes when the world outside fades for a while. Whether we’re sharing a slice of cake, lingering over a glass of wine, or just enjoying the calm of being side by side, this trip gives us something rare—space to breathe, reconnect, and rediscover the ease and warmth of simply being “us” again.
